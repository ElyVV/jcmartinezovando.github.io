Welcome!

I am a Bayesian Statistician and Data Scientist passionate about examining and solving real life problems using data and statistical methods. For years I have worked on different problems in the Social Sciences, in areas like macroeconomics, economic growth, monetary policy, stochastic prediction, causality, official statistics, supervised and unsupervised classification methods, and inference on finite populations using survey data. Part of my professional and academic experience is summarized in this [link].

Currently, I work at the Department of Actuarial Science and Insurance at ITAM as a Full Time Professor in Actuarial Statistics, where I develop research on Bayesian statistics and applications to quantitative risk assessment and management. This is the link to my [official website]

In general, I am interested in studying different aspects concerning the Bayesian paradigm to inference, with particular emphasis in Nonparametric Methods, Time-series Modelling, Actuarial Statistics, Quantitative Risk Modelling, Macro-Econometrics, Computational Statistics, and any aspect of Bayesian statistics that aim at solving real life problems. I am also interested in some contemporaneous topics related to Data Science, such as Visualization of Complex Data and Scalability of Computational Procedures.

I strongly believe in contributing to the development of human capital and general knowledge. Therefore, at ITAM, I happily take part with the planning and delivering of courses related to aggregate-loss modeling, ruin and credibility theory, statistics, and data science.
